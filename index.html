Rehab Nazzal

Palestinian-born multidisciplinary artist working across video, photography, sound, and installation. Her practice examines the effects of settler-colonial violence on people, land, and non-human life, often through quiet, observational images and field recordings.

Current Focus

  • Vibrations from Gaza — short documentary exploring how Deaf children experience bombardment through vibration and tremor rather than sound; an embodied account of place, fear, and resilience.
  • Driving in Palestine — years-long photographic project and book documenting the politics of mobility, surveillance infrastructure, and everyday life along West Bank roads (2010–2020).
  • We, the Wild Plants and Fruit Trees — images and fieldwork tracing non-human lives—wild flora, orchards and trees—within landscapes of dispossession.

Selected Highlights

  • Book: Driving in Palestine (Fernwood, 2023).
  • Film: Vibrations from Gaza (2024) — screenings, talks, and community events.
  • Exhibition: Invisible — multi-channel video and photographic installation addressing incarceration and surveillance.
