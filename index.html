Projects

Film · 2024 · 16 minutes

Vibrations from Gaza

A short documentary that centers Deaf children in Gaza who perceive bombardment through vibration rather than sound. Born and raised under the 17-year-long siege and frequent Israeli military assaults, children Amani, Musa, Israa and others provide vivid accounts of their experiences through sensing vibrations in the air, tremors in the ground, and echoes of collapsing buildings. Winner of Best Short Film at the 2024 BFI London Film Festival, Prix Iris for Best Short Documentary, and Cinema Politica Audience Choice Award.

Photography · Book · 2010–2023

Driving in Palestine

A decade-long photographic study produced from inside moving vehicles across the West Bank. The series records checkpoints, watchtowers, walls, signage, and small gestures of daily life, assembling a road atlas of constraint and endurance. Published by Fernwood Publishing in 2023, featuring 160 black and white photographs with hand-drawn maps and critical essays in English and Arabic by Palestinian and Canadian scholars including Mohammed El-Kurd.

Photography · Installation · Ongoing · 2022–

We, the Wild Plants and Fruit Trees

An ongoing series of photographs and notes tracing the lives of wild flora and cultivated trees within militarized ecologies. Through images of fig, olive, pomegranate, cactus, za'atar, and other plants, the project explores how plants bear witness to land use, enclosure, displacement, and care. Featured in The Willful Plot at the Morris and Helen Belkin Art Gallery, the work explores botany as a form of memory and resistance.

Photography · Environmental

Dead Sea Study

Shoreline studies at the Dead Sea examining extraction, subsidence, and environmental transformation. The work documents the dramatic changes to the landscape caused by mineral extraction and water diversion, tracing the visible marks of environmental crisis.

Installation · 2014

Invisible

A multimedia installation engaging with incarceration and political prisoners, featuring four videos and 1,700 photographs. Exhibited at Karsh-Masson Gallery in Ottawa City Hall, the installation was publicly condemned by Israel's ambassador to Canada, sparking significant public discourse about censorship and Palestinian art. One video documented experiences in Bil'in where activists were attacked during protests. Another featured over 127 rapidly flashing images of Palestinians who were extrajudicially assassinated.

Documentary · 2015

Canada Park

Documentary examining Canada Park in Israel, built on the ruins of three Palestinian villages—Imwas, Yalo, and Beit Nuba—destroyed in 1967. The work exposes how colonial infrastructure obscures displacement and erases Palestinian history through the creation of recreational spaces.

Ongoing Project

Sonic Torture

A sound-animation project focusing on the use of sound and music in the torture of political prisoners, examining how sonic warfare is deployed as a tool of settler-colonial violence and psychological control.

Solo Exhibitions

Invisible — Karsh-Masson Gallery, Ottawa City Hall (2014)

Multimedia installation featuring four videos and 1,700 photographs addressing incarceration, political prisoners, and extrajudicial killings. The exhibition included images of Palestinian prisoners in Israeli prisons and artwork portraying the West Bank and Gaza. Publicly condemned by Israel's ambassador to Canada, Rafael Barak, the exhibition sparked significant public discourse about censorship and Palestinian representation in Canadian institutions. One video, Bil'in, documented experiences in the village where Palestinians and international solidarity activists were attacked by Israeli forces during weekly protests. Another video, Target, featured over 127 rapidly flashing images of Palestinians who were extrajudicially assassinated, including the artist's brother, killed in Athens in 1986.

Divide — Gallery 101, Ottawa (2010)

Solo exhibition of photographs exploring separation and fragmentation in Palestinian territories.

Group Exhibitions & Installations

The Willful Plot — Morris and Helen Belkin Art Gallery, Vancouver (January–April 2023)

Group exhibition exploring gardens as sites of tension between wild and cultivated, temporal and perpetual, sovereign and colonized. Featured Canada Park (2015) and We, the Wild Plants and Fruit Trees (2022). The exhibition brought together artists' practices to expand the notion of the garden, with Nazzal's photography and film giving agency to wild plants growing through ruined Palestinian villages, connecting people to land even as parks and gardens are created as tools of displacement.

Driving in Palestine — Montréal, arts interculturels (MAI) (September–October 2023)

Installation with photographs, video, sound, and printed matter from the decade-long photographic study.

On Borrowed Time in Gaza: Art in Confinement — Monica Reyes Gallery, Vancouver (November 2021)

Fundraiser and solidarity exhibition featuring approximately 200 works by six Palestinian artists based in Gaza: Maisara Baroud, Majdal Nateel, Ghanem Al-Den, Rufaida Sehwail, Mohammed Alhaj, and Maha Daya. Nazzal carried the artwork in her suitcase through the Rafah crossing. All proceeds supported the artists and art-therapy programs in Gaza. The exhibition represented expressions from life in Gaza by artists defying the brutal Israeli blockade imposed on over two million Palestinians since 2007.

About

Palestinian-born multidisciplinary artist based between Montréal and Bethlehem.

Rehab Nazzal is a Palestinian-born multidisciplinary artist whose work examines mobility, vibration, incarceration, and ecologies of care in Palestine. Working across photography, video, sound, and installation, she explores how structures of control shape sensing and everyday movement. Born in Jenin in the occupied West Bank, Nazzal is currently based between Montréal and Bethlehem.

Her practice centers on the effects of settler-colonial violence on the bodies and minds of colonized peoples, on the land, and on other non-human life. Through documentary approaches and immersive installations, she creates spaces for witnessing and understanding Palestinian experience, often creating immersive environments that represent the struggle of the Palestinian people.

Nazzal's work has been exhibited internationally at institutions including the Morris and Helen Belkin Art Gallery, Karsh-Masson Gallery, Gallery 101, San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, Spanish Institute of Art, Prefix Institute of Contemporary Art, and numerous film festivals and art venues across Canada, Palestine, and globally.

In 2014, her exhibition Invisible at Ottawa City Hall sparked significant public discourse when it was publicly condemned by Israel's ambassador to Canada, Rafael Barak. The controversy brought attention to issues of censorship and Palestinian representation in Canadian cultural institutions.

In December 2015, while photographing a military raid in occupied Palestine, Nazzal was shot in the leg by an Israeli sniper—a stark reminder of the risks faced by those documenting occupation. As she stated, "This was not an accident."

Her 2024 documentary Vibrations from Gaza has received international acclaim, winning Best Short Film at the BFI London Film Festival and the Prix Iris for Best Short Documentary, among other awards.

Teaching & Research

Dr. Nazzal has taught at Dar Al-Kalima University in Bethlehem (as Assistant Professor), Simon Fraser University, Western University, and Ottawa School of Art. She is currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at Concordia University.

As a practicing artist, activist, and educator, she considers critical thinking, engagement with the community and with nature to be central to creative processes.
