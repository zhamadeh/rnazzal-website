Bodies of Work

Projects

Film · 2024

Vibrations from Gaza

A short documentary that centers Deaf children in Gaza who perceive bombardment through vibration rather than sound. The work foregrounds touch, tremor, and the constant presence of drones to rethink how violence is sensed and experienced by those who navigate the world differently.

By focusing on the tactile and vibrational dimensions of warfare, the film challenges conventional understandings of how conflict is witnessed and endured, offering insight into the multisensory realities of life under siege.

Photography · Book · 2010–2023

Driving in Palestine

A decade-long photographic study produced from inside moving vehicles across the West Bank. The series records checkpoints, watchtowers, walls, signage, and small gestures of daily life, assembling a road atlas of constraint and endurance.

Published by Fernwood Publishing in 2023, featuring 160 black and white photographs with hand-drawn maps and critical essays in English and Arabic by Palestinian and Canadian scholars and artists.

Photography · Ongoing · 2022–

We, the Wild Plants and Fruit Trees

An ongoing series of photographs and notes tracing the lives of wild flora and cultivated trees within militarized ecologies. This work examines how plants bear witness to land use, enclosure, displacement, and care.

Through images of fig trees, olive groves, wild herbs, and resilient vegetation growing through ruins, the project explores botany as a form of memory and resistance.

Photography · Environmental

Dead Sea Study

Shoreline studies at the Dead Sea examining extraction, subsidence, and environmental transformation.

Installation · 2014

Invisible

A video and photographic installation engaging with incarceration and political prisoners. The work includes four video pieces and 1,700 photographs forming a field of fragments and testimony.

Exhibited at Karsh-Masson Gallery in Ottawa City Hall, the installation was publicly condemned by Israel's ambassador to Canada, sparking important conversations about censorship, representation, and the politics of Palestinian art in Canada.

Biography

About

Rehab Nazzal is a Palestinian-born multidisciplinary artist whose work examines mobility, vibration, incarceration, and ecologies of care in Palestine.

Working across photography, video, sound, and installation, she explores how structures of control shape sensing and everyday movement. Born in Jenin in the occupied West Bank, Nazzal is currently based between MontrÃ©al and Bethlehem.

Her practice centers on the effects of settler-colonial violence on the bodies and minds of colonized peoples, on the land, and on other non-human life. Through documentary approaches and immersive installations, she creates spaces for witnessing and understanding Palestinian experience.

Nazzal's work has been exhibited internationally at institutions including the Morris and Helen Belkin Art Gallery, Karsh-Masson Gallery, Gallery 101, and numerous film festivals and art venues. In 2014, her exhibition Invisible at Ottawa City Hall sparked significant public discourse when it was condemned by Israel's ambassador to Canada.

In December 2015, while photographing a military raid in occupied Palestine, Nazzal was shot in the leg by an Israeli sniper—a stark reminder of the risks faced by those documenting occupation.
